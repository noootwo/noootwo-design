# Anti-Slop

Noootwo Design should not produce work that looks instantly AI-generated.

## Avoid By Default

- Purple-blue-cyan tech gradients with no brand reason
- Inter-only, system-only, or generic SaaS typography when the brand needs character
- Endless rounded cards and safe drop shadows
- Decorative charts, fake metrics, or filler testimonials
- Emoji or fake iconography standing in for a real visual language
- Generic "future of X" marketing copy
- Layouts that repeat the same card module without hierarchy
- Motion that exists only to look busy

## Prefer Instead

- A clear design point of view
- Fewer but stronger visual decisions
- Typography with hierarchy and intent
- Real product evidence when available
- Surfaces that feel specific to the product's audience and tone
- Negative space and rhythm over ornamental clutter

## Self-Check

Before calling a design ready, ask:

- Would this still look convincing if the logo were removed?
- Does it look like this product, or like the average AI demo?
- Is the strongest visual decision tied to the brief, not to fashion?
